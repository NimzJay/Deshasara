Literature Review

Smart Home System or Intelligent Home means in-house electrical appliance automation and control. Recent technological advances in electronic devices have resulted in a huge number of user-friendly and flexible systems being produced that have promoted smart living technology.

In the research done by Mehedi Hasan, Parag Biswas, MD Islam Bilash and Ashik Zafar of North South University Dhaka, Bangladesh on a Comparative analysis on smart home systems [19], they have compared the smart home developing technologies like DTMF, GSM (smartphone based), speech recognition-based, Zigbee-based, Bluetooth-based, Internet and Wi-Fi-based, and home automation solutions based on Gesture was studied [19].

Research Gap

Generally, smart homes are designed for people who are not disable or not handicapped due to various illnesses and locomotive problems. But for the report of World Health Organization, about 15% of the world's population lives with some form of disability, of whom 2-4% experience significant difficulties in functioning [1]. Elderly people and People with disabilities like immobility, paralysis, deaf and dumb face many difficulties while trying to adapt to this kind of normal smart homes which have not been designed in a way that is user friendly for differently abled people.

Objectives
Main Objective

The main objective of this research is to implement an effective solution: MobiGO in aid of immobile people in their domestic life. Smart home solutions have become a convenient system for everyone in their day-to-day activities. As the existing solutions do not cover every aspect of smart home solutions for immobile people, a new gesturedriven solution for immobile people is to be implemented. Apart from main smart home operations, some other major services are to be provided and they are, activity monitoring and abnormal behavior identification of the patient and IoT socket to be plugged to control the domestic devices.
